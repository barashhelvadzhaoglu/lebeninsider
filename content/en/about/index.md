## About Share Hub EU and My Expat Journey

Hello and welcome to Share Hub EU! I'm **Barash**, and I'm thrilled to share my journey and the wealth of information I've gathered with you. Currently, my family and I call **Munich** ([Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Munich+Germany)) home. By profession, I'm a **Network Security Engineer**, navigating the complex digital landscapes of the modern world. But outside of my professional life, I'm a dedicated father to two wonderful children, aged 1 and 5. This means my days are often a delightful blend of cybersecurity protocols and the joyful chaos of toddler adventures, complete with a double stroller that has taught me invaluable lessons about the accessibility (or lack thereof) of European cobblestone streets.

My personal story is one of significant geographical and bureaucratic transition. I was born and raised in **Turkey**, where I spent the formative years of my life. Later, I obtained **Bulgarian citizenship**, a crucial step that opened doors to new possibilities within the European Union. Eventually, this path led me to **Germany**, where I embarked on a new chapter of living and working. This entire journey, spanning three countries and numerous administrative hurdles, has been a masterclass in perseverance, adaptability, and navigating bureaucratic labyrinths. Share Hub EU was born directly from this rich, often challenging, and always insightful process.

The seeds of Share Hub EU were sown in the hours I spent meticulously researching before my move to Germany. What I discovered was a significant void. While official government websites offered cold, factual summaries, and various online forums provided vague, often anecdotal advice, **first-hand, detailed, and truly honest information was incredibly hard to come by.** This wasn't just about finding facts; it was about understanding the nuances, the hidden pitfalls, and the real-world implications of regulations. I needed to know which specific documents might cause delays during a driving license renewal, or the exact legal reason why my Bulgarian license wouldn't immediately work in Germany, despite both being EU licenses. No one had mentioned that requesting a court interpreter in Germany could be free, or what exactly happened when the Jobcenter misclassified my severance compensation. These critical details, often overlooked or deemed too specific, are precisely what make or break an expat's experience.

This blog exists to bridge that gap. Share Hub EU is my commitment to providing the kind of practical, in-depth, and unfiltered information I wished I had access to. It's designed to be a comprehensive resource, empowering you with knowledge derived from genuine, lived experience. My goal is to demystify the expat journey, offering clear insights into everything from bureaucratic processes to cultural integration, and inspiring you to navigate your own European adventure with confidence.

## Our Experience

The creation of Share Hub EU has been an incredibly personal and evolving journey. When I first embarked on my move to Germany, the sheer frustration of finding reliable, nuanced, and truly helpful information was immense. It was a chaotic mix of official jargon, out-of-date forum posts, and well-meaning but ultimately unspecific advice from friends of friends. I realized there was a gaping hole in the digital landscape for **authentic, detailed, first-hand accounts** – not just "what to do," but "what *actually* happened when I tried to do it." That's when the idea for Share Hub EU took root: to build the resource I desperately wished I had.

Blogging, for me, isn't just a hobby; it's a second mission, intertwined with my primary roles as a **Network Security Engineer** and a father of two. Balancing a demanding career with the joys (and challenges!) of raising a 1-year-old and a 5-year-old, alongside documenting every intricate detail of expat life, is a constant act of juggling. There are days when I write late into the night, fueled by strong Turkish coffee, reflecting on the day's bureaucratic conquest or a particularly memorable family outing. This commitment ensures that every article on Share Hub EU is steeped in genuine experience and a desire to truly help. The analogy of my double stroller navigating cobblestone streets isn't just a quirky detail; it encapsulates the entire philosophy of this blog – tackling real-world obstacles with practical solutions, even if it means finding unconventional routes.

What I've loved most about this process is the opportunity to revisit and formalize the often messy, real-time learnings of expat life. It forces me to distill complex experiences into clear, actionable advice. However, what has sometimes fallen short of my initial expectations is the sheer speed at which regulations can change. While I strive to update posts with relevant year tags (e.g., "2026") and keep information current, the administrative landscapes of three different countries are constantly in flux. This means that while the core experience remains invaluable, some minor details might need independent verification against the latest official sources. My goal is to provide a robust framework of understanding, recognizing that some bureaucratic sand will always shift.

Living in Munich with my family significantly influences the content I produce. Our experiences with the **Kita system** ([Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Kita+Munich)), exploring Bavaria with the **Deutschlandticket** ([Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Deutschlandticket)), and even just the daily rhythms of **shopping at local markets** ([Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Munich+local+market)) provide a wealth of material. My articles on childcare, schooling, and family-friendly travel are directly informed by our life here. I want to assure parents and prospective parents that many of the travel guides, especially those for day trips from Munich, are explicitly written with accessibility for strollers and young children in mind. I know the struggle of finding suitable places that cater to little ones, and I share what genuinely works.

Ultimately, my vision for Share Hub EU is to be more than just a blog; it's a community, a shared space for connection and mutual support. It's a place where you can find honest guidance, feel understood, and perhaps even laugh at the absurdities of expat life. I believe that by sharing our collective experiences, we can make the journey for future expats smoother, less stressful, and ultimately more rewarding.
